Saudi Arabia Business Law and Regulations

LEGAL FRAMEWORK:
Saudi Arabia operates under a dual legal system combining Islamic Sharia law with modern civil and commercial regulations. The Commercial Court system handles business disputes with specialized judges trained in commercial law.

COMPANY FORMATION:
1. Limited Liability Company (LLC): Most common for foreign investors
2. Joint Stock Company (JSC): For large-scale operations
3. Branch Office: Extension of foreign parent company
4. Representative Office: For market research and liaison

FOREIGN OWNERSHIP REGULATIONS:
- Most sectors allow 100% foreign ownership
- Strategic sectors may require local partnership
- Free zones offer 100% foreign ownership
- Manufacturing sector: Up to 100% foreign ownership
- Service sector: Generally allows foreign investment

INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY PROTECTION:
- Trademark Law (2002): Comprehensive protection
- Patent Law (2004): 20-year protection period
- Copyright Law (2003): Automatic protection
- Industrial Design Law (2004): 15-year protection
- Saudi Authority for Intellectual Property (SAIP)

CONTRACT LAW:
- Freedom of contract principle
- Written contracts recommended for business
- Arabic language requirement for official documents
- Electronic signatures legally recognized
- Arbitration encouraged for dispute resolution

LABOR LAW:
- Saudi Labor Law (2005): Comprehensive framework
- Work permits required for foreign employees
- Minimum wage regulations
- Employment contract requirements
- Termination and severance regulations
- Worker's compensation mandatory

DISPUTE RESOLUTION:
- Commercial Court: Primary jurisdiction for business disputes
- Board of Grievances: Administrative disputes
- Arbitration: Encouraged for commercial matters
- Mediation: Alternative dispute resolution
- Recognition of foreign arbitral awards

INVESTMENT INCENTIVES:
- Tax holidays for certain projects
- Customs duty exemptions
- Land allocation for industrial projects
- Training and development support
- Research and development incentives

REGULATORY BODIES:
- Ministry of Commerce: Company registration
- General Authority for Foreign Investment (GAFI)
- Saudi Standards, Metrology and Quality Organization (SASO)
- Communications and Information Technology Commission (CITC)
- Capital Market Authority (CMA)
